Tips for Classifying Bibcodes


In general, any record with an affiliation that is clearly listed as part of the Center for Astrophysics | Harvard & Smithsonian or either of the CfA's observatories (meaning the Smithsonian Astrophysical Observatory or the Harvard College Observatory) should be included in the CfA Bibliography.

Common variations include (but are not limited to):

  • Harvard-Smithsonian Center for Astrophysics
  • Center for Astrophysics Harvard &amp; Smithsonian
  • Center for Astrophysics Harvard and Smithsonian
  • HS Center for Astrophysics
  • SAO-Harvard Center for Astrophysics

Exclude

In general, these affiliations should be excluded from the CfA Bibliography, unless the affiliation also mentions the CfA specifically:

  • Department of Physics, Harvard University
  • Department of Earth and Planetary Science, Harvard University
  • Harvard University
    • Exception: Christopher Stubbs is a member of the CfA and the Harvard Dean of Sciences; he often simply uses "Harvard" or "Harvard University" as his affiliation.

Include

In general, these affiliations should be included into the CfA Bibliography:

  • Department of Astronomy, Harvard University
  • Institute for Theoretical Atomic, Molecular and Optical Physics (ITAMP)
  • Institute for Theory and Computation (ITC)
  • Fred Lawrence Whipple Observatory

Check Carefully

In general, these affiliations need to be evaluated carefully. These groups are collaborations that include the CfA. Some members of these collaborations are NOT part of the CfA, and therefore not everyone with this affiliation can be included in the CfA bibliography:

  • Black Hole Initiative

When in Doubt...

If you aren't sure what to pick, than use the "unknown" button. It is better to use "unknown" than to accidently include a record into the Bibliography that does not belong there.

Using the "unknown" button will move the record into a category for checking up on later, or for rechecking the paper to see if clarifying affiliation information has been added.
